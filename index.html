MY INDIA, MY CONSTITUTION

Value Of Republic Day

Republic Day celebrates India's Constitution adoption in 1950, making it a sovereign republic, emphasizing democratic values, equality, and unity. It honors freedom fighters' sacrifices, showcases national strength through parades, and reminds citizens of their rights and duties, fostering patriotism and commitment to a just, progressive nation.

• Significance:

Marks India becoming a republic with its own Constitution, symbolizing self-governance.

• Values:

Upholds democracy, liberty, equality, and the unity in diversity of India.The value of republic day is unspeakable.

• Celebration:

Features grand parades, cultural displays, and honors for citizens, reinforcing national pride.We celebrate this day with faith and peace

• Core Message:

A day for reflection on sacrifices and a renewed pledge to uphold constitutional principles for national progress.

Fundamental Right

The Right Of Freedom

Fundamental rights of freedom, particularly in the Indian Constitution (Part III, Articles 19-22), guarantee essential liberties like free speech, assembly, movement, association, residence, and profession, protecting citizens from state overreach and forming pillars of democracy alongside other rights like equality, religion, and against exploitation, ensuring individual liberty and a secular state. These rights are legally enforceable and crucial for personal development and a democratic society.

Responsibility Of citizen

By following the law, voting, protecting the environment, helping each other, paying taxes, respecting diversity, and continuing to learn, we can make India a stronger, more peaceful nation. Together, we can all play our part in making India a great place for everyone!
